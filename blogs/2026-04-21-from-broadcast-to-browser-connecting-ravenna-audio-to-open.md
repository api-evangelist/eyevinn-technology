Modern broadcast facilities are full of audio. Mixing consoles, stage boxes, playout systems, radio codecs, hardware intercom matrices — they all produce audio that lives on the studio network, moving around as professional audio-over-IP streams. At the heart of this is Ravenna and its interoperable sibling standard AES67 — the de facto standard for studio-grade audio transport over IP.
